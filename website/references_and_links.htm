Class Text

There is no required text for this class - you can find everything you need online somewhere.

3d Graphics

  The the 'standard' rendering text is

Alan Watt
3D Computer Graphics, Third edition
Addison Wesley, 2000
ISBN 0201398559

The graphics 'standard everything graphic' 3D graphics text is

Foley, vanDam, Feiner, and Hughes
Computer Graphics Principles and Practice, Second Edition
Addison Wesley, 1990
ISBN 0201121107

Probably the most understandable text on ray tracing (though it is a bit dated) is:

Andrew Glassner
An Introduction to Ray Tracing
Academic Press, 1989
ISBN 0122861604

And for the whole of image synthesis is (I think I'd recommend this above the 'standard' Watt text):

Andrew Glassner
Principles of Digital Image Synthesis
Morgan Kaufmann Publishers, 1994
ISBN 1558602763

There are numerous other 3D graphics texts.  Among the most helpful for discovering practical solutions to common and specific problems in graphics programming is the Graphics Gems series.  While I have all of them on my bookshelf, and recommend them if graphics is your primary programming focus, they are overkill for this course.

Rogers and Adams
Mathematical Elements for Computer Graphics, 2nd Edition
McGraw-Hill Publishing Company, 1990
ISBN 0070535302

Eugene Hecht and Alfred Zajac

(what I use for reference - unfortunately now out of print.  If you are really interested in a copy of the relevant reflection model and colour science parts, let me know and I'll see what I can work out)

Spectral data for materials and lights:

http://www.cs.utah.edu/~bes/graphics/spectra/ - This page was done by Brian Smits, formerly student at Cornell, post-doc and research faculty at U of Utah, now at Pixar.  It also points you to other sources for spectral data.

Visualization

The best discussions of information presentation and visualization that I have found are by Edward Tufte in his series of books "The Visual Display of Quantitative Information", "Envisioning Information", and "Visual Explanations".  The first is quite general, and in my opinion, the best of the series (though I really liked the last one too).  The last has a lot related to computer generated visualization and explanation - some really great material.  All are available at Powells.  Tufte self publishes.  Check his site at: http://www.edwardtufte.com

Periodicals and Articles

Eric Haines has published Ray Tracing News for a number of years - a really good reference on things that are happening in ray tracing in the late 80's through to now.  There is a pretty good search facility at this source, plus a table of contents and all the back issues.

Periodicals you might check out:

ACM Computer Graphics (SIGGRAPH proceedings)

IEEE Computer Graphics and Applications

Visual Computer

Articles that seem timeless (in chronological order as they relate to class):

"A Characterization of Ten Hidden-Surface Alogorithms", Ivan E. Sutherland, R. E. Sproull, and Robert A. Schumacker, Computing Surveys, Vol.6, No.1, March 1974. -- In spite of the date, this survey discusses the sorting techniques that are the basis for determining what is seen in nearly every renderer you will ever encounter. Interestingly, at the time ray tracing was seen as impractical because of the high computation cost.

"An Improved Illumination Model for Shaded Display", Turner Whitted, Communications of the ACM, Vol.23, No.6, June 1980. -- THE ray tracing paper.
